## Objective

The objective of this session was to understand how phishing attacks work, how attackers use social engineering techniques to deceive users, and what security measures can be taken to identify and prevent such attacks.

---

## Introduction

Phishing is one of the most common cyberattacks used to steal sensitive information from users. Attackers often create fake websites, emails, or login pages that closely resemble legitimate services in order to trick victims into revealing credentials or personal information.

During this training session, I studied the concepts behind phishing attacks and explored how cybersecurity professionals analyze these threat from a defensive perspective.

---

## Topics Learned

### Phishing Attacks

Phishing is a form of cybercrime where attackers impersonate trusted entities to obtain confidential information.

Common targets include:

* Usernames
* Passwords
* Banking details
* Personal information
* Authentication codes

The primary goal of phishing attacks is credential theft and unauthorized access to user accounts.

---

### Social Engineering

Social engineering involves manipulating individuals into performing actions or disclosing sensitive information.

Common techniques include:

* Fake emails
* Fraudulent login portals
* Impersonation attempts
* Urgent security notifications
* Fake customer support communications

Rather than exploiting software vulnerabilities, social engineering exploits human trust and behavior.

## Understanding Phishing Simulations

A phishing simulation is a controlled environment used to demonstrate how phishing attacks are structured and how users may be targeted.

The exercise helped in understanding:

* How phishing campaigns are organized
* How fake login interfaces imitate real websites
* Common warning signs of phishing attempts
* Methods used to protect users from credential theft

---

## Identifying Phishing Attempts

Some common indicators of phishing include:

* Suspicious website URLs
* Misspelled domain names
* Unexpected requests for login credentials
* Urgent or threatening messages
* Requests for confidential information
* Emails from unverified sources

Recognizing these indicators is an important step in preventing security breaches.

---

## Defensive Strategies

To reduce the risk of phishing attacks:

* Verify URLs before entering credentials
* Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
* Avoid opening suspicious links
* Check HTTPS certificates
* Confirm sender authenticity
* Report suspicious emails and websites

These practices significantly improve personal and organizational security.
