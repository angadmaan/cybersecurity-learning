## What I Learned About Phishing

Phishing attacks are designed to trick users into revealing sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, banking details, or personal data.

One important takeaway from today's session was that phishing attacks often target human psychology rather than technical weaknesses.

Attackers commonly use:

- Fake login pages
- Spoofed websites
- Fraudulent emails
- Social media messages
- Urgent security alerts

Many phishing pages look almost identical to legitimate websites, making them difficult for non-technical users to identify.

---

## Why People Fall for Phishing

A successful phishing attack usually relies on trust and urgency.

Some common tricks used by attackers include:

- Creating realistic-looking websites
- Using familiar company logos and branding
- Sending urgent messages that create panic
- Asking users to verify accounts immediately
- Hiding suspicious-looking URLs behind shortened or disguised links

This helped me understand why cybersecurity awareness is just as important as technical security controls.

---

## Phishing Prevention Tips

Some important defensive practices discussed during the session:

- Always verify website URLs before logging in.
- Avoid clicking unexpected links.
- Check the sender's identity carefully.
- Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
- Look for spelling mistakes and suspicious domains.
- Report suspicious messages whenever possible.
