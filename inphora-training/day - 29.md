## Overview

Today's session introduced the concept of **phishing attacks**, one of the most common forms of social engineering used by cybercriminals. Instead of exploiting software vulnerabilities, phishing attacks target human psychology by tricking users into revealing sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, and financial details.

To better understand how phishing attacks are created and how they can be identified, we explored **PyPhisher**, an open-source phishing awareness tool available on GitHub. The objective of this session was purely educational—to understand how phishing attacks work so that security professionals can recognize, prevent, and defend against them.

- How attackers imitate trusted websites.
- Why users should carefully verify URLs before entering credentials.

# What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cyber attack in which an attacker pretends to be a trusted organization or individual to convince victims to reveal confidential information.

The attacker typically imitates legitimate websites, emails, or login pages.

The goal is to steal:

- Usernames
- Passwords
- Credit card information
- OTPs
- Personal information
- Session tokens

Phishing is considered a **social engineering attack** because it exploits human trust rather than technical vulnerabilities.

---

# Why Phishing is Dangerous

Unlike many technical attacks, phishing does not require exploiting software bugs.

Instead, it relies on convincing people to voluntarily provide sensitive information.

Successful phishing attacks may result in:

- Account compromise
- Identity theft
- Financial loss
- Data breaches
- Unauthorized system access
- Malware infections

Because every organization has users, phishing remains one of the most successful cyber attack techniques.

---

# Types of Phishing Attacks

Several forms of phishing exist, including:

### Email Phishing

Fake emails designed to appear legitimate.

### Spear Phishing

Targeted attacks against a specific individual or organization.

### Whaling

Phishing attacks targeting executives or senior management.

### Smishing

Phishing through SMS messages.

### Vishing

Voice-based phishing using phone calls.

### Clone Phishing

Copying legitimate emails and replacing links or attachments with malicious ones.

# Indicators of a Phishing Website

Common warning signs include:

- Misspelled domain names
- Suspicious URLs
- Missing HTTPS certificates
- Urgent or threatening language
- Poor website design
- Requests for confidential information
- Unexpected login pages

Recognizing these indicators significantly reduces phishing risk.

# Ethical Responsibility

Cybersecurity professionals must always use offensive security tools responsibly.

Tools like PyPhisher should only be used:

- In personal labs
- During authorized penetration testing
- For cybersecurity awareness training
- In educational environments

Unauthorized phishing campaigns are unethical and illegal.
